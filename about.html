Frequently Asked Questions

Signs that your home may need repointing include crumbling or cracked mortar, water damage, dampness inside, gaps in mortar, and bulging or loose bricks. If you notice any of these, it’s a good idea to get an inspection by a professional.

Repointing may be needed every 20-30 years, depending on factors like the building’s age, the mortar type, and exposure to weather conditions. Homes in harsh climates may require more frequent maintenance.

While DIY repointing is possible for smaller areas, it’s recommended to hire a licensed mason for larger or more complicated jobs. Professionals have the right tools and experience to ensure quality and safety.

The time required for repointing depends on the size of the project, but it can range from a few days to a couple of weeks. Your mason can give a more accurate estimate after evaluating the project.
